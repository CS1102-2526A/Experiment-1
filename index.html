Let's explore a classic acid-base reaction between hydrochloric acid (HCl) and sodium hydroxide (NaOH). When HCl and NaOH are combined, these two chemicals react in a neutralisation reaction and form sodium chloride (NaCl), which is commonly defined as table salt, and water (H₂O). The chemical equation is:

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

Using Litmus as an Indicator

But how can we observe these chemical changes obviously? Since the results, like colours, of acid-base reaction are often clear, turbid or colourless, we can use a pH indicator to examine the chemical changes.

One of the pH indicators commonly used is Litmus Paper. The colour of the litmus paper will change based on whether a solution is acidic or alkaline.

Litmus solution turns red in acidic solutions, blue in alkaline solutions, and stays constant in neutral solutions. There are three common litmus papers: Red Litmus Paper, Blue Litmus Paper and Purple Litmus Paper.

Here is a table of the changes in colour of the above three litmus papers when they face acid, alkaline and neutral solutions:

Red Litmus Paper Blue Litmus Paper Purple Litmus Paper
Acid Solution Red Red Red
Alkaline Solution Blue Blue Blue
Neutral Solution Red Blue Purple
